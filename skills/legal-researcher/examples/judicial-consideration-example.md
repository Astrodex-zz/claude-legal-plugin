# Judicial Consideration Example

## Scenario
User asks: "Has the case *ASIC v Healey* [2011] FCA 717 been overruled or distinguished?"

## Verification Process

**1. Identify Case:**
- *Australian Securities and Investments Commission v Healey* (2011) 196 FCR 291; [2011] FCA 717
- Federal Court (Middleton J)
- Directors' duties case (s 180 Corporations Act)

**2. Why Judicial Consideration Matters:**
This is a leading case on directors' duties, often cited in advice. Must verify it remains good law before relying on it.

**3. Check AustLII "Cases Citing This Case":**

**Access:** www.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/cases/cth/FCA/2011/717.html

**Click:** "Cases Citing This Case"

**Result:** 150+ citations found

**4. Sort by Date (Most Recent First):**

**Recent Cases (2020-2024):**
- [2024] FCA 45 - "Applied" *Healey* principles
- [2023] NSWSC 234 - "Followed" *Healey* approach
- [2023] FCA 123 - "Considered" *Healey* in detail
- [2022] VSC 456 - "Applied" *Healey* test

**Pattern:** Consistently positive treatment

**5. Filter for High Court:**
- No High Court cases citing *Healey*
- High Court has not overruled or questioned it
- HCA silence is neutral (case not reached that level)

**6. Check for Negative Treatment:**

**Search for keywords:** "overruled", "not followed", "doubted", "distinguished"

**Result:**
- Distinguished: 5 cases (all on narrow factual grounds)
- Not followed: 0 cases
- Overruled: 0 cases
- Doubted: 0 cases

**Example of "Distinguished":**
*Smith v XYZ Ltd* [2022] FCA 89 - Distinguished *Healey* because that case involved non-executive directors, whereas *Healey* involved both executive and non-executive. Court noted *Healey* remains authoritative for its context.

**Analysis:** Distinction is narrow and doesn't undermine *Healey*'s authority.

**7. Cross-Check with Jade.io:**

**CaseBase shows:**
- Applied: 87 times
- Considered: 45 times
- Distinguished: 5 times (narrow grounds)
- Cited: 30+ additional references
- Overruled: 0
- Doubted: 0
- Not followed: 0

**Judicial Consideration Graph:** Shows consistent positive treatment over time

**8. Review Pattern Analysis:**

**Types of Cases Citing Healey:**
- Directors' duties cases (s 180, 181)
- Corporate governance matters
- Professional negligence by analogy
- Officer liability cases

**How Courts Use It:**
- As authority for "reasonable steps" test
- For approach to assessing director conduct
- For analysis of reliance on management
- As example of breach of s 180

**Trend:** Usage increasing over time, not decreasing

**9. Check Appeal Status:**
- *Healey* appealed to Full Federal Court
- Appeal dismissed: *Healey v ASIC* [2011] FCAFC 136
- Full Court affirmed Middleton J's decision
- No further appeal to High Court

**Status:** Affirmed on appeal, never disturbed

**10. Assess Current Status:**

**Conclusion: GOOD LAW - Leading Authority**

**Evidence:**
- ✅ Never overruled
- ✅ Affirmed on appeal
- ✅ Consistently applied for 13+ years
- ✅ No High Court criticism
- ✅ No pattern of being distinguished
- ✅ Frequently cited as authoritative
- ✅ Treatment remains positive in recent cases
- ✅ Leading case on s 180 duties

**Limitations:**
- First instance decision (though affirmed on appeal)
- Facts specific to large public company board
- Should be read alongside other directors' duties cases
- Some factual scenarios may be distinguished

## Key Lessons

**1. Pattern Recognition:**
- 150+ positive citations vs 0 overrulings = good law
- Consistent application over 13 years = established principle
- No HCA criticism = authority unchallenged

**2. Understanding "Distinguished":**
- 5 cases distinguished (out of 150+ citations) is normal
- Read the distinctions - are they undermining or just factual differences?
- Here, distinctions were narrow and didn't question principle

**3. Multi-Source Verification:**
- AustLII showed basic citation list
- Jade provided clearer categorization and trends
- Both sources confirmed same conclusion
- Cross-verification increases confidence

**4. Complete Picture:**
- Check original case
- Check if appealed (and outcome)
- Check subsequent treatment
- Assess overall pattern
- Consider trends over time

**5. Documentation:**
- Record all sources checked
- Note verification date
- Provide URL to case and judicial consideration
- State current status clearly
- Include any limitations or qualifications

This example demonstrates thorough judicial consideration analysis, showing how to determine whether a frequently-cited case remains authoritative or has been undermined by subsequent decisions.
