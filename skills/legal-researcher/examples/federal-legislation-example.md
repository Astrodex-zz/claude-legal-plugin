# Federal Legislation Verification Example

## Scenario
User asks: "Please verify section 180 of the Corporations Act"

## Verification Process

**1. Identify the Legislation:**
- Act: Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)
- Section: s 180
- Topic: Director's duties of care and diligence

**2. Access Primary Source:**
- URL: www.legislation.gov.au
- Search: "Corporations Act 2001"
- Select current compilation

**3. Current Version Details:**
- Compilation No. 140
- As at 1 January 2024
- Includes amendments up to Act No. 141 of 2023

**4. Locate Section 180:**

```
180 Care and diligence—civil obligation only

(1) A director or other officer of a corporation must exercise their powers and discharge their duties with the degree of care and diligence that a reasonable person would exercise if they:
    (a) were a director or officer of a corporation in the corporation's circumstances; and
    (b) occupied the office held by, and had the same responsibilities within the corporation as, the director or officer.
```

**5. Check Amendment History:**

From end notes:
- s 180 inserted by Corporate Law Economic Reform Program Act 1999
- Commenced 13 March 2000
- Amended by Treasury Laws Amendment (2017 No. 1) Act 2018 (gender-neutral language)
- No substantive amendments since insertion

**6. Verify Still in Force:**
- ✅ Section currently in force
- ✅ No scheduled repeal
- ✅ No pending amendments noted

**7. Cross-Check with AustLII:**
- Access www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/ca2001172/
- Section 180 matches legislation.gov.au exactly
- AustLII shows extensive case law applying s 180

**8. Verification Output:**

```
Legislation: Corporations Act 2001 (Cth)
Section: s 180(1)
URL: www.legislation.gov.au/C2004A00818/latest
Version: Compilation No. 140, as at 1 January 2024
Verified: 4 February 2026
Status: Current - in force

Exact Wording:
"A director or other officer of a corporation must exercise their powers and discharge their duties with the degree of care and diligence that a reasonable person would exercise if they:
(a) were a director or officer of a corporation in the corporation's circumstances; and
(b) occupied the office held by, and had the same responsibilities within the corporation as, the director or officer."

Amendment History:
- Inserted: 13 March 2000
- Last amended: 2018 (minor, gender-neutral language)
- No substantive changes to test since insertion

Notes:
- Objective test of "reasonable person" in the circumstances
- Considered alongside s 181 (good faith), s 182 (improper use of position), s 183 (improper use of information)
- Extensive case law on application (see ASIC v Healey, Centro cases, etc.)
```

## Key Points

- Always use legislation.gov.au as primary source for Commonwealth Acts
- Check compilation number and date
- Verify exact wording (don't paraphrase)
- Review amendment history for context
- Confirm section is in force
- Cross-check with AustLII for completeness
- Document all verification details including URLs and dates
